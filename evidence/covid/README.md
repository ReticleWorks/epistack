# COVID case evidence

## What the supplied record establishes

- The panel used ten source documents whose canonical text totals 69,774 words. The [source manifest](source-manifest.json) records public URLs, retrieval metadata, filenames, word counts, and SHA-256 fingerprints.
- The ten complete files were divided once among three reviewer roles: market and natural-origin evidence, skeptical and lab-origin analysis, and methodological audit.
- Each role returned a source-cited judgment. The market review treated the concentration of early known cases around Huanan as moderately strong but not conclusive evidence. The skeptical review gave it weak-to-moderate weight because early case-finding and the spatial analysis may have favored the market. The method review assigned some weight but found it materially weakened by the same concerns.
- The practical candidate claim was how much of the observed geographic concentration survives correction for how early cases were found.
- The preserved source panel has SHA-256 `087bec7ebad58c717553e9751a5367cbb6984cc5302c94e5c3054fc801a26897`. A sanitized public fixture derived from that panel is included with the [runnable Crux demonstration](../../demo-crux/README.md).

## Boundaries

This was a designed workflow exercise, not a survey of expert belief. The reviewers received different source slices and shared model infrastructure. The source set is not balanced by source standing: five sources were peer reviewed, and four of those supported or synthesized the market or zoonotic account. The original run also applied one default source label, so its automated confidence bands do not grade differences in source quality.

This material does not resolve COVID origins. The full source texts are not redistributed in this public appendix. The manifest supplies URLs and fingerprints so a permitted copy can be checked against the source record.
