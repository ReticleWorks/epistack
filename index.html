Epistack

A research workflow that keeps the reasoning, not just the answer.

Finding the claim a contested conclusion rests on — and testing whether its support is as independent as it looks.

Begin walkthrough → Read the proposal
2 case runs · COVID origins, Large Hadron Collider safety 1 predeclared comparison · Perplexity Deep Research Standalone Crux demo · reproducible in one command

Epistack is a human–AI research workflow for contested or technically layered questions. It builds on a hybrid RAG system and preserves the working state beneath a research synthesis: separate source-cited AI judgments, a proposed load-bearing research claim, and typed documentary relationships—shared authors, data, advisers, methods—each tied to its exact supporting passage.

This addresses a specific gap in commercial deep-research tools: a fluent narrative can hide which reviewer weighted which evidence, whether several citations represent separate support or one related body of work, and which observation would most change the picture. The output is a versioned case record another investigator can challenge, extend, or correct without recreating the whole inquiry. The walkthrough that follows exercises the workflow on two case runs (COVID origins, Large Hadron Collider safety) and one predeclared comparison with a commercial deep research tool.

  • Separate judgments — positions, reasons, citations

Epistack adds

The two new pieces

A deterministic component that names the load-bearing claim, and a typed audit that records how documents actually depend on one another.

  • Deterministic Crux comparison — compares preserved judgments
  • Typed relationship audit — records links supported by passages
Output

Case record

A versioned artifact another investigator can challenge, extend, or correct without recreating the whole inquiry. Reasoning stays legible as models and evidence change.

  • Separate judgments preserved
  • Extracted Crux with change conditions
  • Typed relationships with exact passages
  • Revision history

The pieces are combined as a case workflow. The supplied materials do not establish a single consolidated challenge-and-revision interface.

How it works

One case proceeds through six stages.

Each stage leaves an inspectable artifact, so a later investigator can see where a result came from and where a later stage stopped.

  1. 01

    Narrow the question

    Narrow a broad topic to a question whose central claim can be supported, challenged, or left unresolved.

  2. 02

    Build a traceable corpus

    Build a traceable corpus with stable identifiers, retrieval records, exclusions, and hashes.

  3. 03

    Retrieve for separate reviewers

    Use Atlas retrieval to supply evidence to separate reviewers, preserving reasons, citations, counter-evidence, uncertainty, and change conditions.

  4. 04

    Propose the Crux

    Run the crux extractor to propose the load-bearing claim and record how each judgment bears on it. The proposal remains open to acceptance, revision, or rejection.

  5. 05

    Trace typed relationships

    Trace that claim through citations, data, authors, advice, institutions, methods, and assumptions. Keep the type and exact passage for every accepted relationship.

  6. 06

    Publish a versioned case

    Publish the artifacts as a versioned case record so later sources, objections, corrected relationships, or replacement judgments do not erase the earlier state.

Epistack · Problem and approach 02 / 07

A polished synthesis can hide the state of the inquiry.

What disappears inside one narrative

  • The load-bearing claimWhich specific claim carries the conclusion?
  • The independence of supportDo several sources share authors, data, advice, methods, or upstream analyses?
  • The change conditionWhat evidence would alter the current judgment?

What Epistack preserves

  1. Build a traceable corpus and record exclusions.
  2. Keep source-cited judgments separate before synthesis.
  3. Compare those judgments to propose a concrete Crux.
  4. Record source relationships only with supporting passages.
  5. Package evidence, objections, missing evidence, and revisions as a case.

Epistack · Perplexity Deep Research comparison 06 / 07

The comparison is about the research artifact.

One instructed Deep Research run received the same COVID question and ten-source starting set under a protocol fixed before the call.

Deep Research produced the better single narrative.

  • It returned a concise 258-word synthesis.
  • When directly asked, it named the tension between transmission signal and ascertainment bias.
  • It found useful shared-authorship and debate relationships.

Epistack preserves a different record.

  • Separate judgments, cited passages, and the proposed Crux remain inspectable.
  • Relationship types and corrections remain attached to source evidence.
  • The comparison audit corrected an unsupported expansion from shared authorship to a shared-dataset claim.

Limit: one instructed run does not establish typical product behavior or an overall Epistack advantage.
